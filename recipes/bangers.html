Bangers and mash with red onion gravy

Bangers and mash is a traditional British dish consisting of sausages (bangers) served with mashed potatoes (mash) and a rich onion gravy. It is a popular and classic comfort food, often found on pub menus. The name "bangers" comes from British slang for sausages, which historically would "bang" or burst during cooking due to their high water content.

ingredients

  • 30g butter, melted
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 8 thick pork sausages
  • 1 large red onion, cut into thick wedges
  • 1/4 cup salt-reduced traditional gravy powder
  • 1/3 cup red wine
  • 1 cup Massel beef stock
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

Steps

  1. Place potato in a saucepan. Cover with cold water. Bring to the boil over high heat. Boil for 15 minutes or until tender. Drain. Return to pan over low heat. Toss for 1 minute or until excess liquid has evaporated. Add milk and butter. Mash until smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a large heavy-based frying pan over medium heat. Add sausages. Cook, turning, for 10 minutes or until cooked through. Transfer to a plate. Cover to keep warm.
  3. Return pan to medium heat. Add onion. Cook, stirring, for 8 minutes or until softened. Add gravy powder. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until combined. Remove from heat. Gradually stir in combined wine and stock. Return to heat. Cook, stirring, for 5 minutes or until mixture boils and thickens slightly.
  4. Divide mash among plates. Top with sausages, gravy and parsley. Serve.
